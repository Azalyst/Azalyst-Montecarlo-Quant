HOW THIS WAS BUILTMONTE-CARLO

One strategy the data earned. Reverse-engineered from 301,472 real prop-firm trades: every trader-month was replayed through the exact FundingPips 2-step rules. The finding — traders fail on the 5% daily line, not the target; 12.5% of the biggest winners would have busted. The separator between passers and busters is worst-trade size, not style. So the whole edge is risk control.

Instrument: Gold (XAU/USD H1) — the only instrument whose edge survived both halves of a training window and a held-out out-of-sample test (PF 1.46, +27.5%, 7.5% max DD). A single rule across 9 instruments loses; FX & indices over-fit.

Entry: momentum breakout (within 0.05·ATR of the prior 20-bar high in an uptrend). Exit: 2·ATR stop → break-even at +1R → 3·ATR trail → 48-bar time stop. Sizing: 0.75% (P1) / 0.50% (P2), calibrated by a 2-step Monte-Carlo: ~88% full-pass, ~0% daily-bust.

Method & figures: MASTER_REPORT · PLAYBOOK. Paper-traded live on GitHub Actions vs the exact FundingPips rules. The ~88% reflects a favourable gold regime — research deliverable, not financial advice.
